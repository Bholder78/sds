When do you need one?

Any time a chemical is transferred out of its original container — a spray bottle, a bucket, a squeeze bottle, a small mixing can — the new container needs a label, unless the same person who filled it uses it all within their own shift and it never leaves their control.

⚠ An unlabeled spray bottle is one of the most common citations in a plant inspection — and the most dangerous thing in the shop when someone finds it a week later.

The 2 things OSHA requires (HazCom 1910.1200(f)(6))

1
Product identifier The name exactly as it appears on the SDS and the original container — so anyone can match the bottle to the right sheet. Write it big.
2
Hazard information Words, pictures, symbols or a combination that tell the user what can hurt them — signal word, GHS pictograms, hazard statements, or NFPA/HMIS ratings all count. Our template covers all of these, so a filled-out sticker is always compliant.

Filling out our sticker, step by step

1
Product name & date Top of the label. Use the exact SDS name and today's date.
2
Cross out pictograms that do NOT apply The sticker shows all nine GHS diamonds — put a big ✕ through every one that is not on the chemical's SDS, so only the real hazards remain.
3
Check the signal word DANGER (severe) or WARNING (less severe) — it's at the top of the SDS, Section 2. Never both.
4
NFPA / HMIS numbers Health / Fire / Reactivity, 0 (none) to 4 (extreme). Usually printed in Section 16 of the SDS. If the SDS doesn't give them, leave blank.
5
Hazard & precautionary statements Copy the key lines from Section 2 — "Highly flammable liquid," "Causes serious eye irritation," "Keep away from heat," etc.
6
Check the required PPE From Section 8 of the SDS — glasses, gloves, respirator, apron, boots.

Quick rules

  • Marker must be permanent and legible; label must survive the chemical it's next to.
  • If the container is too small, a tag or the sticker on a string is fine.
  • Never use food or drink containers for chemicals — labeled or not.
  • Faded or unreadable label = no label. Redo it.
  • These labels are for workplace use only — shipping containers need full GHS shipping labels.
